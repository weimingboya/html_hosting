Detailed Analysis

Features & Functionality ★★★★★ 5.0

BJCP Styles offers highly detailed, industry-standard style guidelines covering beer, mead, and cider, with granular specifications for all sensory aspects and commercial benchmarks per style. Comprehensiveness and accuracy are exceptional, and resources support competitive judging and education. However, functionality is focused on categorization and documentation, not interactive features or reporting.

Ease of Use & User Experience ★★★★☆ 4.5

Guidelines are organized for straightforward navigation and lookup, with consolidated PDFs and web menus plus supplemental study guides and reference materials. The user-oriented structure serves both judges and enthusiasts effectively. However, the depth of content may be overwhelming for some, and there is no interactive app interface.

Personal Value & Community Engagement ★★★★☆ 4.5

BJCP provides strong personal growth opportunities and deeply engaged community experiences through certification pathways, mentorship, and official events. It excels for those seeking recognition and advancement in beer judging but is less oriented towards casual social networking or broad enthusiast interactivity.

Pricing & Value Proposition ★★★★★ 5.0

The style guidelines are priced affordably at $39, and BJCP-branded merchandise is non-profit, offered at cost with customization and periodic discounts. This creates a transparent and highly accessible value proposition for judges and serious enthusiasts, though guideline access is still paid and shipping delays may affect merchandise.

Performance & Reliability ★★★★★ 5.0

BJCP Style Guidelines are extremely reliable in providing accurate, up-to-date reference data for competitive judging, maintained by a committee and adopted globally. Timeliness of updates and consistency are top-tier; minor market lag does not impact core reliability.

Support & Resources ★★★★★ 5.0

Official BJCP resources include detailed study guides, FAQs, and ingredient references, all maintained with regular updates. The support system is educationally robust and highly effective for exam prep, onboarding, and ongoing learning for certified judges and merchants.

Untappd delivers a highly intuitive interface, mobile-first management options, and state-of-the-art AI-driven menu design for ease of use. Team management and permissions are simplified. Personal Value & Community Engagement ★★★★★ 5.0

Merchants benefit from powerful marketing, unique badges and awards, and data-driven event curation. While beer-centric venues get maximum exposure, broader food/drink merchants must upgrade to Premium for full community tools.

Pricing & Value Proposition ★★★☆☆ 3.5

Untappd’s tiered pricing is aligned with feature access and actual ROI, providing tangible value via analytics-driven growth for larger operators. Steep price increases and strict tier limitations (especially for Essentials) make affordability and value perception challenging for small venues. Lack of public price transparency and frequent price hikes further reduce score.

Performance & Reliability ★★★★★ 5.0

Untappd delivers top-level reliability, data scale, and technical performance. Its analytics for venue, menu, and engagement tracking are exceptionally robust, with real-time data accuracy and broad export options. Sophisticated reporting may require extra training but does not detract from overall dependability.

Support & Resources ★★★★☆ 4.5

Untappd offers responsive multichannel support and extensive self-service documentation, including phone, email, and a detailed Help Center. Training materials and onboarding guides are strong. However, documentation volume may overwhelm new users and feature access locked behind active subscription/trial reduces resource accessibility for prospects.

3. How Do Third Parties Use Cookies on the Site?

Third-party companies such as analytics providers, vendors, and advertising networks use cookies to gather anonymous information about your browsing behavior. These cookies help them analyze how users interact with our Site and track which other websites you visit afterward. The collected data allows these third parties to improve their services, deliver targeted advertisements, and gain insights into broader online user activity patterns - all while maintaining your anonymity. This information may also be used to measure advertising effectiveness and optimize future marketing campaigns across different websites you visit.


4. How Can You Control Your Cookies?

You're free to block or remove cookies at any time through your browser's settings—simply delete existing cookies and disable new ones. For step-by-step guidance, check your browser's help pages. Keep in mind, though, that turning off cookies may prevent certain features from working, stop your preferences from being saved, and cause some pages to display incorrectly.
If you'd like to opt out of cookies from specific third parties, follow the opt‑out instructions provided in each provider's privacy policy.


5. Managing Your Cookies

Cookies improve your experience on our Sites and services. If you choose to turn them off, some features may not work correctly.


A. Opting Out of Behavioral Advertising Cookies

  • EU users: Visit the Your Online Choices portal.

  • US users: Go to About Ads' Consumer Choice Page.


B. Turning Cookies On or Off in Your Browser

  1. 5.1 Google Chrome

    • Click the ⋮ menu → Settings → Privacy and security → Site Settings → Cookies and site data.

    • Enable all cookies: Select "Allow all cookies."

    • First‑party only: Select "Block third‑party cookies."

    • Disable all cookies: Select "Block all cookies."

  2. 5.2 Microsoft Edge

    • Click ⋯ → Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies and site data.

    • Choose "Allow sites to save and read cookie data" (default) or "Block third‑party cookies."

    • To stop all cookies, turn off "Allow sites to save and read cookie data."

  3. 5.3 Internet Explorer 6–11

    • Go to Tools → Internet Options → Privacy.

    • Move the slider to "Medium" or lower to allow cookies.

    • Slide to "Block All Cookies" to disable them.

  4. 5.4 Mozilla Firefox

    • Open ☰ menu → Settings → Privacy & Security.

    • Under "Enhanced Tracking Protection," choose Standard (allows cookies), Strict (blocks many cookies), or Custom to fine‑tune.

    • Or under "Cookies and Site Data," toggle "Manage Exceptions" or "Delete cookies and site data."

  5. 5.5 Opera

    • Menu → Settings → Advanced → Privacy & security → Site Settings → Cookies and site data.

    • Toggle "Allow sites to save and read cookie data" on or off.

  6. 5.6 Safari (macOS)

    • Safari → Preferences → Privacy.

    • Under "Cookies and website data," choose "Always block" or "Allow from websites I visit."

  7. 5.7 Other Browsers

    • Check your browser's Help section or contact its support for cookie settings.


Note:
Most browsers accept cookies by default. If you disable them, certain areas of our Site may be inaccessible, preferences won't be saved, and personalized content might not display.
