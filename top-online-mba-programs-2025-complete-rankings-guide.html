Illinois Gies demonstrates exceptional academic rigor, comprehensive accreditation (AACSB for both college and Accountancy), and a modern curriculum strongly rooted in traditional business disciplines. The program’s breadth covers core business areas (finance, marketing, accounting, management), supported by distinguished faculty and innovative approaches such as experiential learning and critical thinking projects. The only marked limitations are a slightly narrower set of niche interdisciplinary offerings and possible dilution of personalized academic attention due to large online enrollment.

Flexibility & Learning Experience ★★★★★ 5.0

Gies excels in flexibility with 100% online delivery and multiple entry points. The stackable credential design, self-paced learning, pay-as-you-go tuition, and course structure favor working professionals and those with non-traditional schedules. Experiential elements and interactive modalities accommodate varied learner needs, though some may miss campus or in-person experience.

Career Impact & Alumni Network ★★★★★ 5.0

The alumni network is large and highly engaged, largely centered in Chicago but effective nationwide. Gies grads excel in accounting, consulting, and business sectors, with strong career services and numerous placements at top firms. The network's impact is clear in donations and continued engagement. Career outcomes are strong, particularly in traditional fields, though representation in cutting-edge tech and entrepreneurship is less visible.

Tuition & Value ★★★★★ 5.0

Gies provides industry-leading ROI for online degrees, which are competitively priced (most master’s options under $30k). The pay-as-you-go model and certificate stacking enhance flexibility and affordability. On-campus programs are much costlier, presenting higher barriers especially for international students. Overall, the program’s value is outstanding online but moderate for specialized or campus-based degrees.

Student Support & Resources ★★★★☆ 4.5

Gies delivers broad student support and distinguishes itself with AI-powered teaching assistants and modern LMS platforms. Comprehensive academic and IT resources are in place for online learners, and new programs/credentials are continually added. However, IT support is limited during weekends, and the rapid scaling of support may challenge individualization for a growing student body.

Reputation & Employer Recognition ★★★★☆ 4.5

Gies holds a venerable position in U.S. business education, with recognized program outcomes and strong employer favorability, particularly for the iMBA and accounting. Employer ties are most robust in Chicago and mainstream sectors, with less global brand visibility compared to HBS, Wharton, or MIT Sloan. The college enjoys positive feedback for preparing graduates for real-world roles and scores well in national rankings.

ASU W.P. Carey demonstrates exceptional academic quality, evidenced by top national rankings (Online MBA #6, Undergraduate Business #24) and AACSB accreditation. The curriculum is highly diverse, with 13 BS and 23 BA majors, plus notable specializations such as Supply Chain Management (top 10 nationally). Innovative structures allow for interdisciplinary studies and concurrent degrees, supported by a large, experienced faculty. Although the school’s size sometimes limits ultra-personalized experiences and strict entry requirements may be a barrier, the depth, flexibility, and quality of instruction place it in the top tier.

Flexibility & Learning Experience ★★★★★ 5.0

W.P. Carey excels in flexibility, with fully online, evening, and hybrid program options tailored to working professionals and international students. Asynchronous learning and advanced planning allow for effective time management. The ability to mix minors/certificates and build customized education tracks is industry-leading. While there is a required on-campus visit for some online MBAs, small class sizes and team-based learning support an intimate and dynamic learning environment. Minor scale-driven caveats and in-person requirements for remote students are the primary limitations.

Career Impact & Alumni Network ★★★★★ 5.0

The school provides outstanding career support, with a robust Career Services Center offering job coaching, workshops, and proactive employer partnerships across six continents. Alumni network numbers 130,000+ globally, facilitating lifelong mobility and transitions. Employment rates are among the highest for business schools. While niche industry placement could be improved and the large network may be impersonal at times, overall graduate outcomes and professional mobility are top-level.

Tuition & Value ★★★★☆ 4.5

W.P. Carey offers competitive tuition for Arizona residents and delivers accredited, high-value programs with proven career outcomes. Financial aid is reasonably available, including federal loans and assistantships, though the latter require a significant weekly commitment and are limited in number. Nonresident and international tuition rates are substantially higher, and the required campus visit for online programs imposes additional costs. While overall value is strong, affordability may be a challenge for some groups.

Student Support & Resources ★★★★☆ 4.5

Student support services are comprehensive, with advising, tutoring, technical support, and social/professional engagement opportunities. Dedicated faculty advisors and platforms like W.P. Carey Connect enhance accessibility. Career and social events further benefit students, with special initiatives for first-year MBAs. However, the large student population can occasionally stretch personalized advising, and online-specific support responsiveness lacks detailed feedback. Nonetheless, the overall infrastructure is robust and well-suited to diverse student needs.

Reputation & Employer Recognition ★★★★★ 5.0

ASU W.P. Carey is highly ranked and widely recognized by employers, with strong placement rates, a global alumni presence, and favorable recruiter ratings (Wall Street Journal #5 for recruiter preference). AACSB accreditation and extensive employer partnerships reinforce its brand strength. While industry-specific reputation may vary, the school’s overall national and international presence offers strong employability and prestige for graduates.

1. What Are Cookies?

A cookie is a small text file stored in your web browser that allows the Site or third parties to recognize you as a visitor (not personally identifiable). Cookies serve key purposes, including:

Enabling core website functions

  • Collecting usage analytics

  • Storing your preferences

  • Delivering targeted ads and behavioral advertising

Types of Cookies

  • Session Cookies: Temporarily stored and deleted when you close your browser.

  • Persistent Cookies: Remain in your browser until they expire or you manually delete them. Expiration periods vary (minutes to years), as defined within each cookie.

  • First-Party Cookies: Placed directly by our Site.

  • Third-Party Cookies: Placed by external services (e.g., advertisers, analytics providers).


Pixel Tags (Web Beacons)

We may use transparent pixel tags (also called web beacons or clear GIFs) alongside cookies to:

Track user navigation paths (e.g., from our Site to partner websites)

  • Measure ad performance and referrals

  • Support retargeting campaigns (as outlined in our Privacy Policy)

  • Facilitate interest-based advertising (see Section 5 for details)

B. Turning Cookies On or Off in Your Browser

  1. 5.1 Google Chrome

    • Click the ⋮ menu → Settings → Privacy and security → Site Settings → Cookies and site data.

    • Enable all cookies: Select "Allow all cookies."

    • First‑party only: Select "Block third‑party cookies."

    • Disable all cookies: Select "Block all cookies."

  2. 5.2 Microsoft Edge

    • Click ⋯ → Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Manage and delete cookies and site data.

    • Choose "Allow sites to save and read cookie data" (default) or "Block third‑party cookies."

    • To stop all cookies, turn off "Allow sites to save and read cookie data."

  3. 5.3 Internet Explorer 6–11

    • Go to Tools → Internet Options → Privacy.

    • Move the slider to "Medium" or lower to allow cookies.

    • Slide to "Block All Cookies" to disable them.

  4. 5.4 Mozilla Firefox

    • Open ☰ menu → Settings → Privacy & Security.

    • Under "Enhanced Tracking Protection," choose Standard (allows cookies), Strict (blocks many cookies), or Custom to fine‑tune.

    • Or under "Cookies and Site Data," toggle "Manage Exceptions" or "Delete cookies and site data."

  5. 5.5 Opera

    • Menu → Settings → Advanced → Privacy & security → Site Settings → Cookies and site data.

    • Toggle "Allow sites to save and read cookie data" on or off.

  6. 5.6 Safari (macOS)

    • Safari → Preferences → Privacy.

    • Under "Cookies and website data," choose "Always block" or "Allow from websites I visit."

  7. 5.7 Other Browsers

    • Check your browser's Help section or contact its support for cookie settings.
